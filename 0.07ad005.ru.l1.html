Gold medal for Mirosavljev in Munich

Wed, 05/29/2013 - 16:58 -- MRS

Serbian shooting representative Nemanja Mirosavljev has won the gold medal at the World Cup in Munich, in the event small caliber rifle, three positions. His result was 454.5 points; the second-laced Norwegian Ole Kristian Bryhn (453.9); while Swiss Marcel Buerge ended up in the third place (441.6). Previously, Serbian competitor Andrija Zlatic has won two medals in Munich – one gold and one silver.

Branko Ćopić – a magician of emotions and words
This year, we celebrate the centennial of the birth of one of the greatest Serb and Yugoslav writers and poets, Branko Ćopić. As a writer, Branko Ćopić is popular among, and loved by, adults and children alike. In his honour, a ceremony marking the anniversary has been held in the Serbian Academy of Sciences and Arts.
Sweden wins Eurosong – Serbia ranked tenth
Sweden has won the 60th Eurovision Song Contest, with 365 points, the runner-up is Russia, with 303 points, while Italy is at the third position, with 292 points.
A exhibition on Serbian manuscripts missing in the First World War
An exhibition of old Serbian manuscripts that disappeared in the First World War, but have been found meanwhile, has been opened in the National Library of Serbia. The authors of the exhibition are Jasmina Nedeljković and Jovana Stanojlović.
Peter Handke – an honorary citizen of Belgrade
On the occasion of the Ascension Day, Belgrade’s patron saint day, a ceremony was held in the City Hall last night. At the ceremony, eminent Austrian writer Peter Handke was presented the Charter of am Honorary Belgrade Citizen
"Nušićijada" among the best European festivals
A European Commission programme entitled Europe for Festivals, Festivals for Europe(EFFE), initiated by the European Festival Association (EFA) and aimed at creating a European Platform of Festivals, has honoured the Nušićijada theatre festival in Ivanjica, western Serbia, named after one of Serbia’s greatest playwrights, Branislav Nusic, with the EFFE label for 2015 and 2016.
Rafting season started
There are many mountainous rivers with rapids in Serbia, and rafting them is a true adventure. The tourists desiring adrenalized experience on the water are enjoying the thrills of rafting. The season on the Tara river started at the end of April and will last until October.
Unusual fate of icons from Zica
As part of the event “Museums of Serbia – ten days from 10 to 10”, the visitors in the Institute for Study of Cultural Development have a unique opportunity until May 18 to see the original icons from the iconostasis of the Zica monastery, from the time of episcope Ioanicius Neskovic, and to hear the story of their unusual fate.
Borislav Pekic
The literary evening dedicated to the work of one the most significant Serbian writers of the 20th century Borislav Pekic has been held recently n Belgrade. The publishing house Laguna has printed his entire literary opus for the first time, in 14 books.
Wine Day for the first time in Serbia
On May 31, 2015, Serbia will be marking Wine Day (Open Wine Cellars Day) for the first time, with a view to promoting wine tourism. From 10 am to 7 pm, at 13 venues, wine lovers will be able to see the offer and history of more than fifty wine cellars from all over Serbia.
In honor of Mesa Selimovic
The 105th anniversary of the birth of Mehmed Mesa Selimovic was marked, the writer whose literary quotes are filling many notebooks. He was born on 26 April 1910 in Tuzla, graduated at the literary department of the Belgrade Faculty of Philosophy, and until the beginning of the WWII worked as the literature teacher in his hometown. In the downtown Tuzla a monument was erected in his honor.
Protest of IRS employees held in front of Serbian Government
The four-hour calm protest of more than 70 employees of the Radio Yugoslavia – International Radio Serbia was held in front of the Serbian Government building, due to the announced disbanding of the media house that has been working for 79 years and broadcasting in 12 languages.
Monograph Kosovo and Metohija in the Publications of the Ethnographic Institute of the Serbian Academy of Sciences and Arts (1951–1998)
The Etnographic Institute of the Serbian Academy of Sciences and Arts, the Ministry of Culture and Information and the Ethnographic Museum in Belgrade have published a monograph on more than six hundred pages, entitled Kosovo and Metohija in the Publications of the Ethnographic Institute of the Serbian Academy of Sciences and Arts (1951–1998).
As of April 1, "Nostalgia" on Sargan Eight
The museum train "Nostalgia" has begun the summer season on the railway "Sargan Eight", which connects Zlatibor, one of the most beautiful mountains in the west of Serbia, with the Republic of Srpska. The summer season will last until October 31, announced the Serbian Railways.
Old Court soon open for visitors
The edifice of the City Hall, i.e. the Old Court, will be opened for the visitors every Saturday starting in April, after the model of world metropolises, announced Tijana Maljkovic from the Secretariat of Economy
