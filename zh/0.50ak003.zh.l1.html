Conflict Diamonds

What are conflict diamonds?

Conflict diamonds (also known as blood diamonds, converted diamonds, hot diamonds, or war diamonds) have a long and dark history in many regions of Africa. While sales from legitimate diamonds help African countries to prosper and develop, conflict diamonds accomplish the complete opposite. As the name infers, blood diamonds are accountable for the gruesome death of millions of Africans and play a key role in the rise and extension of many civil wars.
Countries such as Angola, Sierra Leon, Liberia, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo have been paralyzed by the vicious cycle of bloodshed that conflict diamonds promote. Rebel groups use the funds gained from blood diamonds in order to purchase arms and fund other illegal activities that perpetuate the violence further. The problem of conflict diamonds is a continuous; however, the UN’s passing of the Kimberly Process Certification Scheme (KPCS) in 2003 has taken a no-tolerance stance on the issue while bringing about greater public awareness. The Kimberly Process joins industry, governments, and civil society in a united effort to ban the trade of blood diamonds and ensure that consumers are not financing war and the violation of human rights.

Avoiding Conflict Diamonds & Diamond Certification

When buying a diamond make sure to work with a trusted jeweler who provides you with authentic certification. A diamond certificate provides important information about your unique diamond such as the 4 Cs, proof of your diamond's identity and value.

Blood Diamond’s War Path

Here are some haunting statistics:

ANGOLA
Civil War: 1961 - 2002
Deaths: More than 500,000 lives
Rebel group - National Union for the Total Independence of Angola (UNITA) - controlled 60-70% of diamond production.

DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC OF THE CONGO
Civil War: 1998 - 2003 (ongoing instability)
Deaths: More than 3.3 million lives
Rebel groups supported by neighboring countries fought for diamonds in the northeast.

SIERRA LEON
Civil war 1991 - 2002
Killed: More than 50,000 lives
Rebel group - Revolutionary United Front (RUF) - mined approximately $125 million of diamonds per year.

LIBERIA
Civil War: 1989 - 1997 & 2000 - 2003
Killed: More than 200,000 lives
Channel for rebel group’s diamonds and arms import.
