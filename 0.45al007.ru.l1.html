Alexander Atamanenko

Alexander Atamanenko, General Director Donbass Arena, Donetsk, Ukraine

Born on 4 November 1976 in Shakhtyorsk, Donetsk region, Ukraine.

In 1996 he received the higher commercial education diploma (major - Marketing Director) issued by the Academic Centre of Management, Business and Law at the International Academy of Sciences (San Marino). In April 2005 he did the Certified Front Desk Representative Diploma at American Hotel & Lodging Educational Institute of the American Hotel & Lodging Association. In February 2008 he successfully completed the Stadium Director full educational course at the Amsterdam Arena training center and EVMI Course with receiving the corresponding certificates. 

Mr. Atamanenko has worked in a managerial capacity since 1997. Starting from 2003, he fulfilled a number of major venue management projects, including fulfillment of the Forest Park Hotel & Resort turnkey project, from designing it to commissioning and managing the operations. Mr. Atamanenko was awarded the Honorable Worker of Ukrainian Tourism Industry title by the Ministry of Tourism of Ukraine.

From February 2008 - throughout the period of FC Shakhtar’s new home stadium construction – he worked in the Operations Director capacity at Stadion Shakhtyor Ltd. After Donbass Arena opening “Grand Show” which was on 29th of August 2009 Alexander Atamanenko became Chief Executive Officer of Donbass Arena Ltd. In 2011 Mr. Atamanenko was involved as programme manager into celebration events dedicated to FC “Shakhtar” 75th Anniversary. The main part of FC “Shakhtar” 75th Anniversary programme was Celebration Ceremony at Donbass Arena, which was at 14th of May 2011.  The Celebration Ceremony won the award for Best Celebratory Event at Eventia Awards in London.

In 2011 Mr. Atamanenko and was distinguished with the ‘For Performance & Merit’ medal by President of Ukraine.

Starting from 2010 Alexander Atamanenko as CEO of Donbass Arena was head of project team during preparation to EURO 2012. In 2012 Donbass Arena hosted 5 matches of EURO 2012 including quarter-final and semi-final and was deservedly recognized as the best host stadium for the most recent UEFA European Football Championship.

Alexander entered ESSMA (European Stadium & Safety Management Association) in 2010, currently being its Board of Advisors Member.
