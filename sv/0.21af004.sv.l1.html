Sclera - is an inflammatory disease of the outer covering of the eye accompanied by pain with the potential loss of views. Scleritis can be

associated with other diseases such as rheumatoid arthritis, Wegener's granulomatosis, lupus erythematous, polyarteritis nodosa, or disturbances due to menstruation.
Scleritis is of three kinds - diffuse nodular and necrosis. It occurs more often in young women.

Sclera - Signs, symptoms and causes

- An autoimmune disorder in a genetically predisposed person can scleritis. This condition is slowly progressive but initially progressive side view irreversible damage. Scleritis not affect both eyes at once which makes the

patient not notice the disease but later lost your sight was affected. Symptoms - strong eye pain, redness, photophobia, decreased vision, tearing.

Diagnostics - shall test visual acuity, slit lamp examination by an ophthalmologist. Other tests include the assessment of intraocular pressure, dilated eye arrogant.


Sclera - Treatment

Scleritis is usually treated with a specific systemic therapy and especially in patients with associated diseases. For non-infectious scleritis immunomodulatory drugs will be prescribed. When it comes to infectious scleritis are prescribed antibiotics for severe cases will use anti-inflammatory drugs. If drug treatment does not respond scleritis surgery may be performed.
